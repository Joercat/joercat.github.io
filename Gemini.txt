This is a major point of failure and performance concern.
 * Speed: USB 2.0 is very slow (max theoretical throughput is 480 Mbps, but real-world transfer speeds are often <30 MB/s). Minecraft servers constantly read and write world data to disk (especially when loading new chunks), and this slow speed will cause significant lag and potentially even server crashes when the world is being accessed heavily.
 * Reliability/Lifespan: USB flash drives have a limited number of write cycles. Running a server from a flash drive, which involves constant reading and writing, will wear it out quickly and greatly shorten its lifespan, leading to data corruption and failure.
 * Recommendation: You must move the server files to a proper SATA Hard Disk Drive (HDD) or, ideally, a Solid State Drive (SSD) if you want reliable and smooth performance. Even a cheap, older SSD would be a massive improvement.
